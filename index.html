What's going on here?

The animation on this page is a representation of a multiplication table for a random factor (lower number, top-right) in terms of a random base (upper number, top-right) -  F  and  B  respectively. The animation iterates over the natural numbers in order starting from one. These numbers up to and including the base value  B  are placed at equidistant points around the perimeter of the circle. Each multiple of  F  is represented by a line connecting the point for the multiplier to the point for the product. The first line drawn is  1 x F  and connects the 1st point on the circle to the  Fth  point. The second line drawn is  2 x F  and connects the 2nd point to the  2Fth  point, and so on. If the product is greater than the value of the base, then the 'number-line' represented by the circle will continue by wrapping around and the number  B + n  will be represented by the  nth  point.
